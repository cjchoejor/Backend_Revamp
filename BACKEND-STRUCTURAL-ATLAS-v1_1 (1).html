Front matter · 01

Preamble

Scope, derivation discipline, and reading instructions for the Atlas as a whole. The preamble is the contract between this document and the implementation team: what is inside, what is outside, and what the counts and findings mean.

Purpose

The Backend Structural Atlas is a single reference surface that expresses the backend in three superimposed readings. It is a derivative document: every count, every entry, every shell is positively derived from DEV-SPEC-001 and reflects what DEV-SPEC specifies, not what Claude or any other tool remembers.

The Atlas exists to give the implementation team one coherent picture of the backend they are building, and to give the Architect one coherent picture of what remains to be deliberated. It does not replace SIGs, DEV-SPEC or Canon. It indexes them.

Derivation discipline

Source-first. Every number in this Atlas was generated by reading the relevant DEV-SPEC-001 section at the time of writing. Memory-based assertions are explicitly disallowed and were not used.

Positive derivation only. Where counts in the session brief or upstream gate assertions differed from what DEV-SPEC specifies, the Atlas records the positive count from DEV-SPEC and surfaces the discrepancy as a finding rather than silently picking a number.

Zero IP provenance. No Canon section numbers, no DOSS principle numbers, no FAC references appear in this document. The Atlas sits downstream of the IP boundary.

Gaps registered, not hidden. Where a catalogue entry cannot be positively derived from DEV-SPEC, the cell is filled with — and a finding is registered in the finding register below.

How to read this document

Start at the Section Index below — it maps the whole document and indicates which sections carry live content and which are pre-built shells.

Layer 1 (§A–§F) lists the twenty-two catalogues. Each catalogue has a header (number, title, source reference, count) and a table or card grid of entries. Counts are authoritative as of this pass.

Layer 2 (§6.1, §6.2) and Layer 3 (§7.1–§7.11) are present as empty shells. Each shell carries a locked anchor ID; Passes 1b and 1c populate the interiors without changing the IDs, the CSS, or the scaffold.

Findings are registered in the preamble finding register at #findings-register. Each finding has an ID of the form ATLAS-FINDING-NNN and a status.

Architectural commentary. Authority in Canon is in-system: GM authority (L3) is the highest operational level but operates within the governed system, not above it — informal GM approval outside system records is FORBIDDEN (Part 1). Escalation (↑) is the only path around a gate — bypassing a stage gate is FORBIDDEN. FORBIDDEN rules (∅) are terminal: they admit no escalation path and apply to all actors uniformly.

Source. All relationships are derived from two sources only: this Atlas's Wall-Chart §6.1 stage-service rows and Dossier Cards (§6.2), and DEV-SPEC-001 Part 6-REV3 §6.5 domain services, §6.6 application services, and §6.7 infrastructure services. No other source files were opened for this section.

Timer path. Cross-domain calls.
