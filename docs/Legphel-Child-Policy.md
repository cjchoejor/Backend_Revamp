# Legphel-Child-Policy

**For:** Reception, front-desk, and duty management  
**Purpose:** A complete statement of how the hotel accommodates, charges for, and cares for children — for staff to apply and to communicate to guests. Built on Legphel's existing age bands. Two items still need the hotel's input, both marked in the text:  
• One open decision — the charge for a child under 11 who takes a separate bed.  
• A few confirm-or-set values — gathered in the Rate Card at the end.

---

## 1. Who is a child

Every guest is classified by age on the date of check-in:

| Category    | Age                |
| :---------- | :----------------- |
| Young child | Under 6 years      |
| Child       | 6 to 10 years      |
| Adult       | 11 years and above |

A guest aged 11 or over is charged as an adult - own bed, full room and full meals. A responsible adult means a parent or guardian aged 18 or over staying in the same room and accountable for the child. Reception may request proof of age where a child's age affects the charge. If a child has a birthday during the stay, their classification is held at the age band on arrival for that stay; the new band applies to future bookings.

## 2. Accommodation and beds

Children under 11 share the existing bedding with their parents/guardians - no extra bed is added. Guests aged 11 and over are given their own bed and charged as adults. A child under 11 stays free in the room only when sharing with at least one full-paying adult. [Open - to confirm: the charge for a child under 11 who takes a separate bed instead of sharing.] Meals for that child still follow the age rule in Section 4.

| Guest                  | Bed            | Room charge             |
| :--------------------- | :------------- | :---------------------- |
| Under 6, sharing       | Shares bedding | Free                    |
| 6–10, sharing          | Shares bedding | Free                    |
| Under 11, separate bed | Own bed        | [Open – to confirm]     |
| 11 and over            | Own bed        | Full room / person rate |

## 3. Occupancy and capacity

Every guest, including children, counts toward the room's maximum occupancy. Reception confirms the room type's maximum before confirming the booking.

- Maximum children per room, required accompanying adults, and whether infants count toward occupancy are set in the separate room-capacity policy, not here, so the two policies stay consistent.

## 4. Meals and food

Meals run on the age band, within whatever meal plan is booked (breakfast only, half-board, full-board):

| Guest       | Meals                      |
| :---------- | :------------------------- |
| Under 6     | Free                       |
| 6–10        | 70% of the adult meal rate |
| 11 and over | Full adult meal rate       |

- The child meal rate applies to the per-person meal component of the plan or package, not the room.
- A child under 6 eats free regardless of the bed arrangement; a 6–10 child is charged 70% whether sharing or in a separate bed.

## 5. Booking and declaration

- All children must be declared at the time of booking — number and ages. This lets reception price correctly and check capacity.
- If guests arrive with undeclared children, reception re-prices the booking and, if the room is now over capacity, arranges a suitable room or an additional room.
- A change in the number of children after booking is re-priced and re-checked for capacity.
- Children and their ages are recorded on the registration card and reflected on the folio.
- For OTA / online bookings, child details from the channel are captured at intake and verified at check-in.

## 6. Supervision and liability

- Children must be supervised by a parent or guardian at all times in all areas of the hotel.
- The hotel is not responsible for unsupervised children.
- Any damage or loss caused by a child is the responsibility of the accompanying parent/guardian and may be charged to the folio.
- Unaccompanied minors: guests under 18 may not book or occupy a room without a responsible adult aged 18+ (confirm Legphel's stance — some properties decline unaccompanied minors entirely).
- A child should not be left alone in a room or hotel area when too young to be safe doing so; the parent/guardian judges and is responsible.

## 7. Special situations

- One adult with several children — a minimum adult-to-child ratio may apply (hotel to set; remove if not used).
- Children of different ages in one room — each child is classified and charged individually by age.
- A child with special needs — note requirements at booking; reception arranges accordingly.
- Youth or school groups — handled under a separate group arrangement, not this policy.
- A child added late to a booking — treated the same as a change in child count (Section 5).

## 8. Privacy and safeguarding

- The hotel handles children's personal information with the same care and retention rules as all guest data.
- Staff are expected to act on any concern for a child's safety or welfare and report it to the duty manager (adopt as a safeguarding statement if Legphel wishes).
- Local legal requirements regarding minors — including ID at check-in — are followed as required by Bhutanese law (confirm specifics locally).

## 9. At the desk — quick procedure

For every booking with children:

1. Ask each child's age (as at check-in).
2. Classify — under 6 / 6-10 / 11+.
3. Beds — under-11s share with parents; 11+ get their own bed (adult). For an under-11 who wants a separate bed, apply the agreed charge [open — to confirm].
4. Check capacity — do the total guests fit the room's maximum?
5. Apply meal charge — free under 6; 70% for 6-10; full from 11.
6. Record — adults, children, ages, and any separate bed, on the booking and registration card.

### Worked examples

- 2 adults + a 4-year-old sharing — room free, meals free.
- 2 adults + an 8-year-old sharing — room free, meals at 70% of the adult rate.
- 2 adults + an 11-year-old — own bed, full room and full meals (adult).
- 2 adults + a 5-year-old who wants a separate bed — meals free; bed charge per the agreed rate [open — to confirm].
