Reference · for the build team

The design system, made visible

The grammar key. Every visual choice on a working screen traces to a rule below — and the operator never sees a stage number or a clause.

1 · Colour is meaning

Warm and calm so the desk never feels like an audit. Each functional colour carries one operational meaning.

2 · Two things about every fact

Each fact tells you where it came from (a corner mark) and how tightly it's held (the frame) — different channels, so they never collide.

Origin — the corner mark

what to do with the fact
✎CapturedAct on it. Typed or chosen by a person.
∑DerivedConsume it. The system computed it — read, don't edit.
◇SuggestedJudge it. Proposed by algorithm or AI.
⚙SystemKnow its origin. The system did this for you.

Binding — the frame

how it may be acted on
WorkingLive. Still changeable.
In transitionCaution. Expired or awaiting approval.
ConfirmedBound. Sealed — a change opens a new round.

Why two channels, not two colours: a confirmed price (bound) was still typed by a person (captured). Mark + frame show both truths without fighting.

Before confirming it's an indicative band on a hatched card. At confirm it stamps to a frozen total the guest is held to.

4 · When it asks you to confirm

Reserved for real consequence, so the prompts that matter never get ignored.

!Pauses you only when an action is irreversible, affects the guest or another staff member, or commits money or a room you can't reclaim — and names that consequence. In this slice, that's only confirming a booking.
Just does it for everything routine — one tap, no ceremony.
